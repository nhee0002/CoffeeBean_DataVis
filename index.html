WORLD COFFEE PRODUCTION

The coffee plant does not thrive across the entire globe Instead, they are suited to grow in conditions with warm, tropical climates and rich soils (Scott, 2015). This region has been termed the 'Coffee Belt' (Scott, 2015).

The top five coffee producers include Brazil, Indonesia, Colombia, Ethiopia and Vietnam. During the time period, Brazil has consistently been the top annual Coffee Producer, producing 30 to 40% of total coffee beans each year. Brazil also shows the most variation in terms of percentage production, while other countries remain relatively constant during this time period.

COFFEE PROUDCTION AND PRICE

Overall, total coffee production and price have increased since 1990. While there are over 50 coffee producing countries, most coffee bean production can be accounted for by the top five coffee producers which include Brazil, Indonesia, Colombia, Ethiopia and Vietnam. During most years, the top five producers produce more than 50% of the coffee.

COFFEE BEAN QUALITY

There are four main coffee species in the world these include Arabica, Robusta Liberica and Exelsa. Arabica and Robsuta are most common (Cafe Direct, 2020)

Overall, quality ratings for Arabica and Robusta are quite high, averaging around 7.5 out of 10 for each quality criteria. However, Arabica seems to have more variance for each of criterias.
